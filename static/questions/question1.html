BRIEFING
The location mainly deals with a planet’s relation to the Goldilocks Zone [an orbital region around a sun in which it falls in the correct area in terms of temperature for carbon-based life to develop]. When the planet is too hot, the gases may heat up until they cannot be contained by the gravity; so shoot off into space, and if it is too cold, some essential gases can liquify and/or become too heavy, creating dense clouds, often poisonous to humans. Furthermore, the temperatures affect liveability. The human body can only deal with small variations from its desired temperature; ~37 ֯ . Therefore, the optimum range is between 15 - 40 degrees.
