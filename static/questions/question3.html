BRIEFING
Magnetosphere is the outer layer of a planet which holds the magnetic field. Any star still undergoing nuclear fusion at its core is constantly emitting solar winds [stream of charged particles (protons, electrons & alpha particles) from the upper atmosphere of the sun] due to intense heat near the surface of the sun, or the corona. If a planet is insufficiently protected by a magnetic field, this bombardment would do severe and near-irreversible damage to any atmosphere [the charged particles hit the gases constantly until they are accelerated to escape velocities and shot off into space in a process called atmospheric stripping] or life developing on the surface. The closest example to home is our red neighbour Mars, which through persistent atmospheric stripping and periodic solar flares [natural phenomenon which increase the star’s brightness and solar winds due to internal reactions for short periods of time] lost it’s almost Earth-like atmosphere, and almost all chance at developing life.
The optimum magnetic field would be over 30 microteslas in order to maintain a long term colony.
