BRIEFING
The mass of a planet also affects habitability as it affects the gravitational force exerted on the surface of the planet, sometimes making it too strong for human requirements, as well as the kinds of gases able to be retained in the atmospheres. An example is Mercury, whose size (in conjunction with spin and temp) make it impossible to hold the heavier gases required in significant quantities.
The optimal mass is 4.26x10^24 – 8.52x10^27.

Di Wie Mass – 9x10^25
Di Ex Mass – 1x10^28
